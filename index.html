This repository accompanies the preprint Recursive Black Hole Cosmology: An Interpretive Framework. The central conjecture: cosmological expansion may be interpreted as the horizon-separated internal description of a black hole, with the dark energy scale inherited from the parent Schwarzschild geometry via Λgeom = 1/Rs². This ansatz was identified during numerical investigation after the preprint was written; it is a candidate realization of the framework's conjecture, not a result derived within it. No new fields or dynamical equations are introduced. All tools are structural consistency checks, not predictive models.

Numerical exploration of the FRW dynamics reveals a narrow parent-mass window (log10 M/M☉ ≈ 22.5) in which the geometric vacuum scale yields ΛCDM-like late-time structure without additional degrees of freedom. This selection effect arises from internal consistency constraints of the FRW sector and is presented as a structural result rather than an observational claim.

Scope
This framework does
  • Provide an interpretive synthesis of BH thermodynamics and FRW cosmology
  • Test geometric compatibility of Λ = 1/Rs² with late-time FRW
  • Identify a narrow parent-mass window producing ΛCDM-like dynamics
  • Implement a bounce as a classical phase boundary (no singularity)
  • Compute epoch transitions (rad-mat eq., mat-Λ eq., zacc)
This framework does not
  • Predict cosmological observables or fit data
  • Derive Λ = 1/Rs² from first principles
  • Provide a microscopic bounce mechanism
  • Model information transfer across the horizon
  • Introduce new fields or dynamical equations (Ec² is a free numerical parameter of the integrator, not a physical constant)
